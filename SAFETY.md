# Silver Shield Safety Guidance

Silver Shield is a warning tool, not a guarantee.

## Important limits

Silver Shield must never claim:

- “This is definitely safe.”
- “This is definitely a scam.”
- “We guarantee protection.”

Silver Shield can miss scams. It can also flag harmless messages. It is designed to slow people down and encourage safer checks.

## Recommended wording

Use careful wording such as:

- Likely safe.
- Be careful.
- High risk signs found.
- Scam-like warning signs.
- Silver Shield can make mistakes.
- When in doubt, ask someone you trust or contact the organisation directly.

## Emergency steps

If money was sent:

1. Contact the bank immediately using the number on the back of the bank card or from the official banking app.
2. Do not use phone numbers from the suspicious message.
3. Tell the bank it may be fraud.
4. Keep screenshots and message evidence.
5. In the UK, report fraud to Action Fraud where appropriate.

If bank details were shared:

1. Contact the bank immediately.
2. Ask whether cards, accounts, or online banking access need to be frozen.
3. Change banking passwords only through the official app or website.

If a password was shared:

1. Change the password from the official website or app.
2. Use a different password if it was reused elsewhere.
3. Turn on two-factor authentication where possible.

If a verification code, OTP, or PIN was shared:

1. Contact the affected organisation immediately.
2. Tell them a verification code or PIN may have been shared.
3. Do not approve any new login, payment, or transfer request.

If remote access was installed:

1. Disconnect the device from the internet.
2. Remove remote access tools such as AnyDesk or TeamViewer if they were installed unexpectedly.
3. Ask a trusted technical person or reputable repair service to check the device.
4. Contact banks and important accounts from a different trusted device.

## Family support principle

Silver Shield should reduce panic, not create it. The safest tone is calm, practical, and specific:

- Pause.
- Do not click.
- Do not pay.
- Do not share codes.
- Ask the trusted contact.
- Contact the organisation through an official route.
